KLM Open

European Tour Golf Event

Every September 40,000+ fans and visitors come to the KLM Open, the oldest golf tournament on the European Tour. To keep all these visitors engaged over the four-day event, KLM Open strives each year to improve the live experience for fans.

A small IBM project team came together for three consecutive years to create an innovative mobile fan experience!

Watching golfers can be boring...


Being at a golf match, it is magical to watch players tee off early morning. But golf has never been a spectator sport like soccer or baseball. Because typically fans have no interaction with the game whatsoever and are not allowed to cheer or make noise. They silently watch players when they tee off and then wait around while the players walk around the course. Fans might catch one or two of their favourite players when they arrive at the last hole, but even that is not certain because it’s hard to predict what time they will be there. Watching and following multiple players is even more challenging. 

Tracking locations

To improve the experience, it was paramount to show fans the real-time location of their favourite players. To achieve this, each group of golfers was equiped with a custom made, cloud connected, GPS tracker that updated their location every 10s. 

Hill Statement


To quickly convey the idea of the solution, we used the Hill Statement from the IBM Design Thinking methodology. By conducting user interviews, with a structured questionnaire, we created a Hill Statement accurately describing the to-be end state of the solution. These interviews were filmed and taped, thus allowing us to capture student-sentiment and make sure we were buidling what was wished for. 

Tracker Design

Designing beacons for accurate location determination

For the solution to work, the beacons needed to send out their ID as frequently as possible. Standard iBeacon configuration defaults to 100ms between transmits. This was too low to reliably detact if a swimmer crossed the virtual tripwire. 

The beacons’ manufacturer Kontakt.io was willing to work with us on a custom firware that would allow for a 25ms transmission interval. This resulted in a reliable mathematical model to calculate a swimmers position relative to the beacons. 

To solve the higher power consumption, the beacons were installed in custom waterproof enclosures with AA Lithium batteries. The result was a net battery lifetime of one year.

Prototyping in the Pool

Trials by the project team

Although first tests were done from behind the desk, testing in-real-life is important. Water causes reflection in the Bluetooth RSSI reading, causing issues with the measured location of the swimmer. 

In the first video trials on the right, the beacons are mounted on the guard-rail seperating the swimlanes. The second video shows the beacons approx a meter above the water in the middle of the swimlane. 

Real life testing

Testing both mounting solutions

From previous trials it was determined that mounting the beacons either above the swimming lane or on the swimming-lane dividers would lead to the most predictable alarms.

These two design-sketches were made on a flight to Helsinki and were later 3D printed to test in the pool.

There were pro’s and cons to both locations, but above the swimlane prevailed. The main argument was that this solution worked independently from from the location in the swimming lane.
